Common questions

Tap a question to open it. These are general ideas you can bring to your own healthcare team.

Preparation usually includes meeting with the surgeon, sharing your medical history, and following any instructions they give (such as when to stop eating or drinking). Always follow the plan your team provides for you.
You might ask about the goals of surgery, how long it takes, what recovery is like, possible risks, and who to contact if you’re worried afterward. Writing questions down before the visit can help.
Recovery time depends on the type of surgery and the person’s health. Only your healthcare team can give you a realistic time frame and specific activity limits.
Many people lean on family, friends, spiritual communities, or support groups. You can also ask your healthcare team about counseling or patient support resources connected to the hospital or clinic.
Always bring your personal questions to your healthcare team. They know your medical situation and can give specific guidance.
