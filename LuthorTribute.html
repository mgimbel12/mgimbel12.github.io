Family Safety Plan • Inspired by the Luthor “what if”

Family Safety Plan

A calm, practical plan for real families—no super‑villains, just everyday courage. Use this as a gentle template and adapt it to your home.

🏠 Safe places & exits
  • Choose two safe rooms in your home where everyone can gather in an emergency.
  • Walk through two exit routes from your home (front door, back door, stairs).
  • Pick a meeting spot outside (a tree, a neighbor’s porch) where everyone reunites.
📞 Contacts & code words
  • Write down 3 trusted adults (family, neighbors, community leaders) with phone numbers.
  • Create a simple family code word that means “I need help, now.”
  • Teach children how to say their full name, address, and city to emergency services.
🧠 Feelings & boundaries
  • Remind everyone: no one is allowed to hurt, threaten, or scare them on purpose.
  • Practice phrases like “I don’t feel safe” and “I need a trusted adult.”
  • Make a list of safe places in the community (school office, library, community center).
🧳 Go‑bag basics
  • Small backpack with water, snacks, flashlight, and a copy of important numbers.
  • Any medications that must be kept nearby, if a doctor has prescribed them.
  • A comforting item: photo, small toy, or note reminding them they are not alone.
🚨 In danger? Call 9‑1‑1 (emergency)
🧭 Need local help? Call 3‑1‑1 (community info, non‑emergency)
This page is not a substitute for professional help. If anyone in your family is being hurt or feels unsafe, reach out to emergency services or a trusted professional right away.
