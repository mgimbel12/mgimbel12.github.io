Phase: Innocence & Longing
“All I ever wanted was for my father to be proud of me.”
Young Lex is not a villain in the making — he’s a lonely child in a hostile environment. Sensitive, intelligent, and desperate for affection, he learns early that love is conditional and safety is fragile. His innocence is real, but it’s raised in a house that punishes vulnerability.

Reflection & Viewer Lens
Lex’s story is a slow rewriting of his own innocence. The boy who wanted to be good is taught, over and over, that power is safer than vulnerability.
  • Notice how often Lex reaches for connection before control.
  • Track the moments where betrayal hardens him instead of healing.
  • Ask where “villainy” is really a defense against pain.
