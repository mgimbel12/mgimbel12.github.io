Recognizing Signs of Abuse or Neglect

  • Abrasions, cuts, or bruises
  • Asphyxiation or hypothermia
  • Untreated bed sores
  • Broken or dislocated bones
  • Burns or scalding
  • Unexplained injuries or wounds
  • Over-sedation or poisoning
  • Over/under medicating
  • Friction from restraints
  • Dehydration or malnutrition

Understanding Abuse and Neglect

Some caregivers may harm children due to:

  • Difficulty managing their own emotions or stress
  • Lack of understanding of child development
  • Unrealistic expectations of behavior
  • Personal history of trauma or abuse
  • Financial hardship
  • Substance use and loss of control

If you or someone you know needs help, text HOME to 741741 to reach a trained Crisis Counselor. Free, 24/7, and confidential.

Local Henderson/Las Vegas, NV Child Abuse & Neglect Hotline: 702-486-6930

Support and Recovery Resources

Doctors can help explain developmental needs and recommend support resources.
Parenting classes and financial assistance may be available through local health departments.
Psychologists, counselors, and social workers can guide families through healing.
Religious organizations often offer counseling, food, and community support.

Examples of Physical Abuse

  • Shaking or shoving
  • Slapping or hitting
  • Beating with objects
  • Burning with matches or cigarettes
  • Scalding with water
  • Pulling out hair
  • Fractures or broken bones
  • Withholding basic needs like food, drink, or bathroom access
