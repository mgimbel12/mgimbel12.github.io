Open Heart Surgery – Simple Guide

A calm, plain-language look at what open heart surgery is, why it may be done, and how people are supported before and after. This is not a “how to” for surgery.

What is open heart surgery?

Open heart surgery is a major operation where surgeons open the chest so they can reach the heart directly. It is used to treat serious heart problems such as blocked arteries, damaged heart valves, or some heart defects present at birth.

Only highly trained heart surgeons and medical teams perform this surgery in a hospital operating room. It is never something a person can or should try to do on their own.

Why might someone need it?

Doctors may recommend open heart surgery when medicines or less invasive procedures are not enough to treat a heart problem. Common reasons include bypassing blocked arteries, repairing or replacing heart valves, or fixing certain heart defects.

Before, during, and after

Before surgery Tests, medical visits, and careful planning with the heart team. Patients learn about risks, benefits, and what to expect.
Day of surgery The person goes to the operating room, receives anesthesia (medicine to sleep), and the heart team performs the operation.
Recovery Time in intensive care, then the hospital, then healing at home with follow‑up visits and cardiac rehab if recommended.
