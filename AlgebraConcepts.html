📘 Core Algebra Concepts

Short explanations that a math teacher can point to quickly.

Variables & Expressions

A variable is a letter that stands for a number. An expression combines numbers, variables, and operations.

Example: 3x + 5

x = unknown 3 = coefficient 5 = constant
Equations

An equation says two expressions are equal. Solving means finding the value that makes it true.

Example: 2x + 4 = 10 → x = 3

Balance Do to both sides
Inequalities

Show when one side is greater, less, or equal/greater than another.

Example: x + 3 > 7 → x > 4

>, <, ≥, ≤ Ranges
Slope & Linear Functions

Linear functions make straight lines. Slope is “rise over run.”

y = mx + b

m = slope b = y-intercept

🌱 Life Circumstances that Use Algebra

Short stories teachers can use to connect math to real life.

Budgeting: A student wants to buy snacks and save money. If they earn $10 a week and spend $4, the expression 10w − 4w shows how much they save after w weeks.
Travel & Distance: A bus travels at a constant speed. Distance can be written as d = rt (distance = rate × time). This helps plan trips, arrival times, and fuel costs.
Sharing & Fairness: Splitting a bill or dividing supplies uses division and fractions. Algebra helps make sure everyone gets a fair share.
Growth & Change: Saving money, growing plants, or tracking progress in sports can be modeled with functions that show change over time.
