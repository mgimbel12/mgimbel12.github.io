World History Eras Civilizations Change Over Time

📜 Major Eras in World History

These eras are big “chapters” that help us organize the story of humanity.

Ancient World
Ancient Civilizations
Early complex societies such as Mesopotamia, Ancient Egypt, Ancient India, China, Greece, and Rome. They developed writing, laws, governments, and early religions.
Middle Ages
Medieval Period
Roughly 500–1500 CE. Feudalism, castles, knights, the rise of the Catholic Church in Europe, and important developments in the Islamic world, Africa, and Asia.
Early Modern
Renaissance & Reformation
A “rebirth” of art and learning in Europe (1300s–1600s) and major religious changes that challenged the Catholic Church and created new Christian denominations.
Modern Era
Industrial & Contemporary World
From the 1700s onward: industrialization, revolutions, world wars, decolonization, civil rights movements, and the rise of global connections and technology.

📘 Key History Terms & Definitions

These words help us talk clearly about events, people, and change.

Civilization
A complex society with cities, organized government, social classes, and shared culture.
Empire
A large political unit that controls many territories and peoples, usually under one ruler.
Revolution
A major, often sudden change in government, society, or technology. Example: the American Revolution.
Colonization
When one country takes control of another land and its people, often for resources and power.
Primary Source
A direct piece of evidence from the time being studied (letters, photos, diaries, speeches).
Secondary Source
A work created later that interprets or explains the past (textbooks, documentaries, articles).
Cause & Effect
How one event (cause) leads to another event (effect) in history.
Human Rights
Basic rights and freedoms that belong to every person, such as life, liberty, and equality.

🌍 Big History Learnings

Questions historians ask when they study the world:

Who?
Which people, groups, or leaders were involved?
What?
What happened? What events, laws, or changes took place?
When & Where?
When in time and where in the world did this happen?
Why & How?
Why did it happen and how did it affect people, both then and later?
