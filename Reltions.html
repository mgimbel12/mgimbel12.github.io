People, Stories & Relationships

Mount Rushmore

Features U.S. Presidents George Washington (b. 1732), Thomas Jefferson (b. 1743), Theodore Roosevelt (b. 1858), and Abraham Lincoln (b. 1809), carved in South Dakota to represent key eras of American history.

National Treasure & Adventure

Adventure stories like National Treasure use American history, symbols, and monuments (including presidents and founding documents) as clues in modern treasure hunts and family‑team problem solving.

Saved by the Bell

A teen sitcom about high‑school friendships and relationships, originally airing in the early 1990s, focusing on school life, humor, and growing up.

Smallville & Superman

Smallville follows Clark Kent’s teenage years in Kansas before he becomes Superman, exploring family, friendship, and destiny. Superman first appeared in comics in 1938 as a symbol of hope and justice.

Barbie, Stacey & Ken

Barbie debuted as a fashion doll in 1959; Ken was introduced as her boyfriend in the early 1960s. Stacey and other friends expand the Barbie universe with different styles, careers, and stories.

Princesses: Cinderella & Snow White

Cinderella and Snow White are classic fairy‑tale princesses whose stories center on kindness, courage, and hope through hardship, often ending in loving, supportive relationships and restored families.

Descendants (D3) – Mal & Friends

In Disney’s Descendants films, Mal (daughter of Maleficent) builds relationships with Ben, Uma, Harry, Gil, and others. Their stories explore trust, forgiveness, and choosing good over evil in friendships and couples.
