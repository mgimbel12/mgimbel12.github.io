Cast of Hearts — Smallville & Verona

In this adaptation, the ancient feud of Montagues and Capulets is reborn as the quiet war between Luthor power and Smallville innocence. Lana stands at the crossroads of both worlds, while Lex wrestles with destiny, legacy, and love.

Lex Luthor
Heir of LuthorCorp • “Romeo” figure
Torn between the shadow of Lionel and the light he glimpses in Lana, Lex becomes our tragic romantic lead. His brilliance and ambition echo Romeo’s intensity, but his world is built on secrets, corporate wars, and meteor mysteries.
Lana Lang Luthor
Smallville soul • “Juliet” figure
Lana carries the heart of Smallville—its barns, storms, and whispered legends. In this retelling, she chooses Lex, stepping into the Luthor name, and becomes the bridge between innocence and empire, love and consequence.
Clark Kent
Silent guardian • Unspoken rival
Clark watches the unfolding tragedy with the weight of secrets only he knows. He is neither Montague nor Capulet, but a quiet force whose choices shape the fate of both Lex and Lana.
Lionel Luthor & Smallville
The feud itself
Lionel, LuthorCorp, and the town of Smallville together become the “ancient grudge.” Corporate towers and dusty roads clash, and Lex and Lana stand in the middle, trying to write a new ending to an old war.

Act I — First Storm

The story opens on a meteor-lit Smallville sky. Lex’s car spins off the bridge, and Clark saves him—our prologue of fate. Lana watches from the distance, unaware that this rescue will bind three lives together.

In the language of Shakespeare, this is the “ancient grudge” reborn: Luthor wealth colliding with farm-town simplicity, and a friendship forming where suspicion should have lived.

Act II — Secret Vows

Lex and Lana begin to see each other not as symbols—Luthor and Smallville—but as two souls trying to outrun their pasts. Their conversations echo balcony scenes: quiet, late-night talks in the loft, in the mansion, in the spaces between storms.

Here, our adaptation imagines a private vow: not a literal marriage yet, but a promise to stand together against the expectations of the town and the Luthor name.

Act III — Broken Trust

Secrets begin to fracture the fragile peace. Lex’s obsession with the truth about Clark mirrors Romeo’s impulsive decisions, but with a sharper edge—corporate investigations, hidden labs, and files locked behind steel.

Lana feels the weight of lies from every side. In classic Shakespeare fashion, misunderstandings and half-truths become daggers sharper than any blade.

Act IV — The Plan

Instead of a sleeping potion, our Smallville version uses disappearance and reinvention. Lana chooses the Luthor name, becoming Lana Lang Luthor, hoping that by stepping into Lex’s world she can rewrite its story from the inside.

Lex, convinced that love can redeem legacy, builds a future around her. Yet every step forward is shadowed by Lionel’s manipulations and Clark’s unspoken truths.

Act V — The Choice

Our fan-based retelling offers two possible endings: a tragic one, echoing Shakespeare, and a hopeful one, unique to Smallville. In the tragic path, Lex and Lana are separated by betrayal and fear, their love buried under the rubble of LuthorCorp.

In the hopeful path, they choose honesty over power. Lex steps away from his father’s empire; Lana refuses to let fear dictate her future. The “star-crossed” lovers become “choice-crossed” instead—proving that destiny can be rewritten.
