Chloe & Lois Safety Plan

This is a gentle, general safety plan inspired by Chloe Sullivan and Lois Lane. It is not medical advice and cannot replace a therapist, doctor, or crisis service. Use it as a calm template to think about how to keep a fun day safe and supportive.

🧠 Feelings check‑in
  • Before the “genius fun day” starts, everyone shares how they’re feeling (excited, tired, worried, etc.).
  • Agree on a pause word (like “time‑out” or “yellow light”) that anyone can use if they feel overwhelmed.
  • Remember: needing a break does not “ruin the day.” It protects the day.
🤝 People & support
  • List at least one trusted adult who knows about the fun day and can be called if emotions get big.
  • Decide who is “team Chloe” (organizing, checking in) and who is “team Lois” (speaking up, asking for help).
  • Make sure everyone knows: it’s okay to say “no” to any activity that feels unsafe or unkind.
🌱 Breaks & coping tools
  • Plan regular breaks for water, snacks, stretching, and quiet time away from screens or puzzles.
  • Keep gentle tools nearby: drawing supplies, music, a favorite book, or a calm corner to sit in.
  • End the day with a “gratitude headline”: one sentence about something kind or hopeful that happened.
📞 Professional & emergency help
  • Write down contact information for a therapist, doctor, school counselor, or mental health clinic if you have one.
  • If you or someone else is in immediate danger, call local emergency services (for example, 9‑1‑1 in the U.S.).
  • Ask a trusted adult to help you find crisis hotlines or local mental health resources in your area.
🚨 In immediate danger? Call 9‑1‑1 (or your local emergency number).
🧭 Need local info? Call 3‑1‑1 where available for community services.
This page cannot diagnose, treat, or replace professional care. If you are struggling with thoughts of self‑harm, harming others, or feel unsafe, please contact emergency services or a mental health professional right away.
