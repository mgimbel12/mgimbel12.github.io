üé≠ Life & Career

Michael Rosenbaum (born July 11, 1972) is an American actor and podcaster best known for portraying Lex Luthor on the television series Smallville. His career spans film, television, and voice acting, including roles in Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 2 & 3, Urban Legend, and Justice League as Wally West / The Flash. He also starred in the TV Land comedy series Impastor and directed the indie film Back in the Day.

üè° Early Life

Born in Oceanside, New York and raised in Newburgh, Indiana, Rosenbaum grew up in a large blended family. His mother, Julie Eckstein, is a writer, and his father, Mark Rosenbaum, worked in pharmaceuticals. He is Jewish and has spoken about feeling more connected to religion in New York than Indiana.

ü¶∏ Lex Luthor & Superhero Legacy

Rosenbaum won a Saturn Award in 2001 for his portrayal of Lex Luthor in Smallville. He also voiced Wally West / The Flash in multiple DC animated series. In a memorable episode of Justice League Unlimited, Lex and Wally switch bodies, allowing Rosenbaum to play both characters simultaneously.

After leaving Smallville in season 7, Rosenbaum returned for the series finale in 2011, reprising his role as Lex Luthor.

üé¨ Directorial Work

In 2014, Rosenbaum made his directorial debut with Back in the Day, filmed in his hometown. He also began work on a comedy titled Sorry is For Sissies, but the project was shelved due to budget issues.
