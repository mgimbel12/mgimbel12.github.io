🌱 Age Ranges – Short Version & Detail

Each card gives a short version first, then a few detailed supports.

0–2 years
Trust, Senses & First Words

Short version: Babies learn safety, love, and how the world feels.

  • Builds trust through warm, consistent care and comfort.
  • Explores with senses: touching, tasting, looking, listening.
  • Begins to babble, say first words, and respond to names.
  • Starts moving: rolling, sitting, crawling, standing, walking.
Safety Attachment Senses
3–5 years
Play, Imagination & Early Rules

Short version: Young children learn through play, stories, and simple routines.

  • Uses imagination in pretend play and storytelling.
  • Learns to share, take turns, and follow simple rules.
  • Builds early language: longer sentences, questions, “why?”
  • Practices fine motor skills: drawing, building, simple crafts.
Play Imagination Routines
6–8 years
Skills, Friends & Confidence

Short version: Children practice school skills and learn how to be a good friend.

  • Grows in reading, writing, and basic math.
  • Understands simple rules, fairness, and taking responsibility.
  • Builds friendships and learns to solve small conflicts.
  • Enjoys games with clear rules and teamwork.
School Skills Friendship Fairness
9–12 years
Identity, Thinking & Responsibility

Short version: Children start to ask bigger questions about who they are and what is right.

  • Thinks more logically and can see more than one side of a situation.
  • Develops a stronger sense of self, interests, and talents.
  • Handles more responsibility at home and school.
  • Friendships become deeper and more important.
Identity Logic Responsibility
13–18 years
Independence, Values & Future

Short version: Teens explore independence, values, and future goals.

  • Thinks more abstractly about justice, beliefs, and big ideas.
  • Builds personal values and boundaries.
  • Balances independence with guidance from caring adults.
  • Begins planning for future education, work, and dreams.
Independence Values Future
