How high is the sky?

1st Level of the height of the sky.

Atmospheric Layers

Troposphere

Troposphere: Extends from the Earth's surface up to about 8-15 kilometers (5-9 miles), depending on latitude and weather conditions. This is where most weather occurs

Stratosphere

Ranges from about 15 kilometers to 50 kilometers (9 to 31 miles) above sea level. It contains the ozone layer.

Mesosphere

Extends from 50 kilometers to about 85 kilometers (31 to 53 miles). This layer is where meteors burn up upon entering the atmosphere.

Thermosphere

Ranges from 85 kilometers to 600 kilometers (53 to 373 miles). This layer contains the ionosphere, which is important for radio communication.

Exosphere

Begins around 600 kilometers (373 miles) and extends to about 10,000 kilometers (6,200 miles). This is where atmospheric particles are so sparse that they can travel hundreds of kilometers without colliding with one another. Factors Influencing Perception:

The perceived height of the sky can vary based on atmospheric conditions, such as clouds and pollution.

The color and clarity of the sky can also change with weather patterns and time of day.
