Lex Luthor as a mirror

In many versions of his story, Lex Luthor is brilliant, wounded, and terrified of losing control. He tries to manage fear with power and distance. This page gently flips that script: instead of glorifying harm, it asks how someone like Lex could build a safety plan—how any of us can notice our warning signs, reach out, and protect the people we care about.

Mental Health Safety Plan • “If Lex chose help”

Lex Luthor Safety Plan (Fictional Lens, Real Care)

This is a gentle, general safety plan inspired by Lex Luthor’s fictional struggles. It is not medical advice and cannot replace a therapist, doctor, or crisis service. Use it as a calm template to think about your own support system.

🧠 Warning signs & feelings
  • Notice personal warning signs: trouble sleeping, constant anger, feeling hopeless, or wanting to isolate.
  • Write down thoughts that feel heavy or overwhelming so they don’t stay trapped in your head.
  • Remember: having big feelings does not make you “the villain.” It means you deserve understanding and support.
🤝 People & places of support
  • List 3 trusted people (family, friends, mentors, community leaders) you can talk to when things feel too heavy.
  • Include safe places: school counselor’s office, clinic, community center, faith space, or library.
  • Practice simple phrases: “I’m not okay,” “I need help,” or “Can we talk for a few minutes?”
🌱 Coping tools (non‑harmful)
  • Gentle activities: slow breathing, stretching, drawing, listening to calm music, or stepping outside for fresh air.
  • Write a short “anchor note” to yourself: reminders of people who care, reasons to stay, and hopes for the future.
  • Keep a small grounding object (photo, bracelet, stone) to hold when emotions spike.
📞 Professional & emergency help
  • Write down contact information for a therapist, doctor, school counselor, or mental health clinic if you have one.
  • If you or someone else is in immediate danger, call local emergency services (for example, 9‑1‑1 in the U.S.).
  • Ask a trusted adult to help you find crisis hotlines or local mental health resources in your area.
🚨 In immediate danger? Call 9‑1‑1 (or your local emergency number).
🧭 Need local info? Call 3‑1‑1 where available for community services.
This page cannot diagnose, treat, or replace professional care. If you are struggling with thoughts of self‑harm, harming others, or feel unsafe, please contact emergency services or a mental health professional right away.
