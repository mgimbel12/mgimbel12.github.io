Subject: Lex Luthor Universe: Smallville / Metropolis

Lex Luthor – The Brilliant Adversary

Lex Luthor is often portrayed as one of the most intelligent humans in the DC universe. In Smallville, he begins as a complicated friend to Clark Kent, torn between genuine loyalty and a growing obsession with power and control.

Across comics and films—including Superman Returns— Lex sees Superman not as a savior, but as a threat to human independence. His genius, resources, and charisma make him a villain who believes he is the hero of his own story.

Alignment: Anti-hero / Villain Core traits: Intellect, ambition, control

The House of El & Allies

Superman, Supergirl, and the Flash each carry a different kind of hope: the quiet farm-boy courage of Clark Kent, the resilient optimism of Kara, and the kinetic, time-bending heart of Barry Allen. Together, they form a counterweight to Lex Luthor’s obsession with control.

Superman
Symbol of hope

Superman, born Kal-El, is the last son of Krypton and raised as Clark Kent in Smallville. His powers—flight, strength, and invulnerability—are matched by a moral compass shaped by his adoptive parents.

To Lex Luthor, Superman is an alien interference in human destiny. To most of Metropolis, he is a reminder that power can be used with humility and compassion.

Alignment: Hero Home: Metropolis
Supergirl
Courage & resilience

Supergirl, Kara Zor-El, arrives on Earth carrying memories of Krypton that Clark never had. Her story often explores identity, grief, and the pressure of living up to the “S” symbol.

Where Lex sees vulnerability as weakness, Kara turns her vulnerability into empathy, protecting people who feel small in a dangerous world.

Alignment: Hero Home: National City / Metropolis
The Flash
Heart & speed

The Flash, often Barry Allen, is the fastest man alive. His connection to the Speed Force lets him outrun bullets, bend time, and race through multiverses.

Barry’s optimism and sense of justice contrast sharply with Lex’s cynicism. Where Lex tries to control the future, the Flash keeps trying to fix it, even when it hurts.

Alignment: Hero Home: Central City
Lex Luthor
Genius & control

In Smallville, Lex begins as a lonely heir searching for connection, especially with Clark. Over time, secrets, fear, and his father’s influence push him toward paranoia and ruthless choices.

In Superman Returns, Lex leans fully into grand, world-altering schemes. His biography becomes a study in how brilliance without empathy can turn into something dangerous.

Alignment: Villain Home: Metropolis / Global

Map quest to the “Treasurement”

Imagine this as a playful quest: you’re moving through a stylized map of Metropolis and Smallville, following clues left by Lex and the heroes. The “treasure” isn’t gold—it’s a reminder that power without empathy is empty, and that hope is the real artifact worth guarding.

Quest steps

  1. Start in Smallville: Picture the Kent farm at sunrise. Ask yourself: what did Clark learn here that Lex never did?
  2. Travel to Metropolis: Follow the path of skyscrapers and headlines. Notice how the city reacts to Superman and to Lex.
  3. Decode Lex’s plan: On the map, his route is sharp and angular—built on control, fear, and spectacle.
  4. Follow the House of El: Their path curves gently, looping back to protect people who fall behind.
  5. Reach the “Treasurement”: At the glowing “X,” you find no weapon, no throne—only a simple idea: the strongest person in the room is the one who chooses to protect, not to dominate.
