🎭 Key Theater Terms

The language that helps us talk about plays and performances.

Play
A story written to be performed by actors on a stage.
Script
The written text of a play, including dialogue and stage directions.
Scene
A smaller part of a play that happens in one time and place.
Monologue
A long speech by one character, spoken to others or to the audience.
Stage
The space where the actors perform.
Audience
The people watching the performance.
Props
Objects used on stage by actors (books, cups, letters, etc.).
Costume
Clothing worn by actors to show who their character is.

🌟 Roles in a Theater Production

Many people work together to bring one story to life.

Actor
The person who plays a character on stage.
Director
The person who guides the actors and decides how the play will look and feel.
Playwright
The person who writes the script.
Stage Manager
The person who organizes rehearsals and makes sure everything runs smoothly during the show.

✨ Theater Learnings

Questions to think about when you watch or create a play:

Story
What is the play about? Who changes and how?
Emotion
How do the actors show feelings with voice, face, and movement?
Space
How is the stage used to show place, time, and focus?
Collaboration
How do all the roles work together to tell one story?
