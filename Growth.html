Learning & Growth • Help-Provided Justice System

A restorative, educational, humane model for responding to mistakes with understanding, support, and repair.

Restorative Justice Learning & Growth Support, Not Isolation
Growth Progress
1. Learning
Understand, don’t shame
Purpose

Understand what happened, why it happened, and who was affected—without labeling the person as “bad.”

Components
  • What happened (the action or choice)
  • Why it happened (feelings, pressures, misunderstandings)
  • Who was affected and how
  • Which expectation, rule, or value was involved
Tools
  • Reflection prompts (“What was I thinking at the time?”)
  • Cause–effect mapping (“When I did X, Y happened.”)
  • “What I understand now” statements
I can clearly describe what happened.
I understand who was affected and how.
I can name the value or rule involved.
2. Growth
Build new skills
Purpose

Turn insight into action by practicing better choices, communication, and problem-solving.

Growth Activities
  • Practice tasks that rehearse a better response
  • Communication or empathy exercises
  • Step-by-step improvement plans
  • Timed goals or countdowns to track progress
Principles
  • Small, realistic steps
  • Encouragement and feedback
  • Visible progress (checklists, levels, or stars)
I have a clear plan for doing better next time.
I have practiced or rehearsed a better response.
I can name at least one new skill I’m using.
3. Help Provided
Support, not isolation
Purpose

Make sure the person is supported, guided, and not left alone with the problem.

Support Types
  • Mentorship or coaching
  • Guided reflection with a trusted adult or peer
  • Peer support or buddy systems
  • Access to tools (vocabulary, math, planning, emotional regulation)
Support Philosophy
  • “We fix this together.”
  • “You are capable of better.”
  • “You are more than this mistake.”
I know who is supporting me through this.
I have at least one tool or resource I can use.
I do not feel alone with this problem.
4. Accountability
Repair, not humiliation
Purpose

Repair the impact of the action in a way that is fair, safe, and respectful.

Restorative Actions
  • Apologies (spoken or written, when appropriate)
  • Fixing or replacing what was damaged
  • Helping the affected person or community
  • Completing a responsibility task that restores trust
Proportional Time-limited Never humiliating
I have taken a concrete step to repair the harm.
The consequence fits what happened (not too big, not too small).
I understand why this accountability step matters.
5. Reward & Reintegration
Recognize growth
Purpose

Recognize learning and growth, restore trust, and welcome the person fully back into the community.

Reward Types
  • Trust restored and privileges returned
  • Certificates, badges, or positive notes
  • XP points, levels, or progress stars
  • New opportunities or responsibilities
Reintegration Elements
  • Clean slate going forward
  • Updated goals and next steps
  • Affirmation of progress and identity
“You grew.” “You learned.” “You belong here.”
I have been recognized for my effort and growth.
I know what my next positive steps are.
I feel welcomed back into the group or community.
Mistake → Learning → Growth → Help Provided → Accountability → Reward → Reintegration

This system treats every mistake as a chance to learn, grow, repair, and return with dignity.
